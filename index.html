About Me:

I am a senior Computer Engineering student at the University of North Texas, with a passion for technology and the outdoors. My curiosity for the fundamental workings of computers began around my junior year of highschool when I assembled my first PC. I chose computer engineering over computer science because my interest lies in making embedded systems that can be deployed into the real world, hopefully towards environmental conservation applications. I was raised in a family that valued time in nature, and as our natural places in this world dwindle by the day, it is my focus to use technology to help monitor and preserve these spaces. While I have no real interest in web development, knowing how to build, create, and host a website is vital to my mission because at the end of the day, the best way to reach people is via the internet. I took this course with the hope of learning how to build basic webpages/ applications, so that I have the skills necessary to make my projects accessible by everyone.

This webpage was developed using a boostrap template for CSCE3420 at The University of North Texas
